summary of what makes a good git commit msg

  1. Keep the subject line to 50 chars or less
  2. Put a space between the subject line and the rest of the msg
  3. capitalize the subject line
  4. use the imperative mood in the subject line
  5. in the message body, talk about what change was made and why it was made, not how it was made
  6. don't end the subject line with a period
  7. wrap lines in the body at 72 characters

so, again, that's:

  1. Separate the subject line from the body with a space
  2. limit your subject line to 50 chars
  3. don't punctuate subject line
  4. use imperative mood in subject line
  5. wrap lines in body text at 72
  6. capitalize the subject line
  7. in the message body, answer why the change was made and what it was made on, vs. how it was made (use source comments for that)
